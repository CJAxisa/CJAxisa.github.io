Backdoor Break-in (2019)

About

The original idea for Backdoor Break-in was to make a 2D platformer that used the mouse to jump. The mouse-based movement gives the game a unique feeling. On top of that, the player can posess enemies. These enemies generally make the player slower but, when the player hops out of the enemy, their first jump will be much faster than usual. This speedboost grows if the player comboes jumping into and posessing enemies. The idea is that the gameplay and control scheme would promote fast and precise gameplay. The mouse based jumping would help facilitate this as it gives players more control of the arc and speed of their jumps.

Development

Backdoor Break-in was made over the course of 6 weeks for a Game Design & Development II class. The control scheme for the game was my idea. I love strange control schemes in games as I feel they help bring genres in new and interesting directions. I was a designer and programmer on this project. We had a group of 5 on this project (4 programmers & 1 artist). I programmed much of the player movement, designed the level, and a lot of the mechanics behind the game. Months after the core development of the game, I spent a few days touching up the project. I felt that we had a strong core and that just a little bit more polish could make the project much more fun. I beleive this paid off and the current version of the game is indicative of what we wanted to accomplish with this project. It's a fast, fun, and quirky game.
